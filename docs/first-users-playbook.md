# Lustre — First Users Acquisition Playbook
**Author:** Chief Commercial Officer
**Date:** March 2026
**Status:** Active — Pre-Revenue Stage

---

## Executive Summary

This playbook covers how Lustre acquires its first 10–25 paying customers. The goal is **not** to scale yet — it is to find the right early adopters, validate willingness to pay, and gather the signal needed to sharpen the product before opening the floodgates.

Because the product is still being polished and billing infrastructure is not yet fully live, we operate in a controlled, founder-led mode. Every early user is hand-picked and hand-held. This is deliberate.

---

## 1. Pre-Outreach Gate: What Must Be True Before Charging

Before a single user is asked to pay, the following must be in place. If it is not, we onboard on a **free basis** and flag the gap for urgent resolution.

| # | Gate | Owner | Status |
|---|------|-------|--------|

| 2 | Trial expiry and subscription enforcement live | Engineering | Pending |
| 3 | User invitation system (multi-seat) working | Engineering | Pending |
| 4 | Password reset flow functional | Engineering | Pending |
| 5 | Quote → PDF → email working reliably | Engineering | Done |
| 6 | Mobile experience acceptable (iOS Safari) | Engineering | In Progress |
| 7 | Basic error handling — no raw 500s visible to users | Engineering | Pending |

**Rule:** If gates 1–4 are not met, run a "Founding Member" programme (described below) at £0 with a clear commitment letter. Do not take card details.

## 2. Target Profile: Who We Are Looking For

We want early users who will give us honest, structured feedback — not people who will churn silently.

### Ideal Founding Customer Profile

| Dimension | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| Business type | Owner-operated cleaning business, UK |
| Team size | 1–15 staff |
| Current tooling | Spreadsheets, WhatsApp, or basic invoicing tools (Xero, FreeAgent) |
| Pain | Losing track of quotes, scheduling in their head, unprofessional client comms |
| Tech comfort | Comfortable enough to use online banking and book on Xero |
| Geography | England (London, Manchester, Birmingham, Leeds prioritised for in-person) |
| Budget signal | Already paying for *something* (even just a spreadsheet add-on) |

### Profiles to Avoid (For Now)

- Businesses with 50+ staff (product is not ready for their complexity)
- Businesses expecting Xero/QuickBooks integration (not built yet — be transparent)
- Anyone who asks about calendar sync or mobile app first
- Businesses expecting white-labelling or custom branding on client-facing docs

## 3. The Founding Member Programme

Rather than a standard free trial, we run a structured **Founding Member** cohort. This creates scarcity, sets expectations correctly, and builds a feedback relationship from day one.

### What Founding Members Get
- 3 months free (or heavily discounted — £19/mo suggested)
- Direct line to the founding team (Slack or WhatsApp group)
- Their feedback shapes the roadmap
- Lock in Starter pricing for life (once billing is live)
- Founding Member badge / acknowledgement (if they consent)

### What We Ask in Return
- 30-minute onboarding call
- One feedback session per month (30 mins, structured)
- Permission to use anonymised data in case studies
- Honest reviews on G2 / Capterra once product is stable

### Cohort Size
- **Cohort 1:** 5–8 users (tight, manageable)
- **Cohort 2:** 10–15 users (once cohort 1 feedback incorporated)
- **Paid launch:** After cohort 2 validation

### Formalise It
Send a simple one-page commitment letter (not a contract) that sets out:
- What they're getting
- What you're asking for
- The timeline to billing
- How to leave if it is not working

This builds trust and sets the right tone.

## 4. Outreach Channels

### Channel 1: Personal Network (Start Here)

The founder's personal and professional network is the highest-conversion channel at this stage. Do this before anything else.

**Actions:**
1. Write a personal LinkedIn post: "I've built something — looking for 5 cleaning business owners to try it free and help shape the roadmap. DM me." Keep it honest and unpolished. Authenticity converts.
2. Message 20–30 people directly. Not a bulk message. Personal notes.
3. Ask every contact: "Do you know anyone who runs a cleaning business?"

**Who to contact directly:**
- Previous colleagues in property/facilities management
- Anyone you know who has hired a cleaner or cleaning company
- Estate agents and letting agents (they refer cleaning companies constantly)
- Property managers

---

### Channel 2: UK Cleaning Industry Communities

**Facebook Groups (search and join these):**
- "Cleaning Business Owners UK"
- "UK Cleaning Professionals"
- "Domestic Cleaning Business Network UK"
- "Cleaning Business Startup UK"

**Approach:** Do NOT post a "sign up for my product" message. Instead:
1. Spend 1–2 weeks engaging genuinely — answer questions, share useful content
2. Post a problem-focused message: *"Running a quick survey — how do you currently manage your client quotes and job scheduling? Spreadsheet? WhatsApp? Something else?"*
3. DM the people who reply with 5+ word answers (they care)
4. Have a conversation, not a pitch

**Reddit:**
- r/CleaningTips (lurk, don't pitch)
- r/smallbusiness (broader — useful for positioning feedback)
- r/UKPersonalFinance (context on pricing sensitivity)

---

### Channel 3: Trade Bodies and Associations

| Organisation | Why | Approach |
|-------------|-----|----------|
| British Institute of Cleaning Science (BICSc) | Largest cleaning industry body UK | Email membership team — ask about partner/affiliate programme or newsletter slot |
| National Carpet Cleaners Association (NCCA) | Specialist but active community | Same approach |
| Association of Professional Independent Cleaners (APIC) | SME-focused | Direct outreach to board members on LinkedIn |
| Federation of Master Cleaners (FMC) | Established, trusted | Newsletter advertising or joint webinar |

**Near-term:** A single newsletter placement or mention at one of these organisations can reach hundreds of the exact right people.

---

### Channel 4: Partnership with Adjacent Tools

Cleaning businesses already use tools for accounting. Partners at those tools have warm lists.

**Priority partners:**
- **FreeAgent** — Popular with UK sole traders and small businesses. Explore integration or co-marketing
- **Xero** — Larger but has an app marketplace. Being listed here is a long-term play but worth exploring early
- **Bark.com** — UK platform where cleaning businesses buy leads. Integration or partnership could be powerful
- **Checkatrade / MyBuilder** — Tradespeople platforms used by cleaning businesses

**Approach:** Contact their partnership or ecosystem teams. Offer a joint blog post, webinar, or a mention in exchange for a mention back.

---

### Channel 5: Cold Outreach (Last, Not First)

Direct cold email or LinkedIn outreach to cleaning business owners. This is lower conversion but scalable once you have a message that works.

**Where to find targets:**
- Google Maps: Search "cleaning company [city]" → most have a website with a contact email
- Companies House: Filter recently incorporated companies with SIC code 81210 (General cleaning of buildings)
- LinkedIn: Search "cleaning business owner UK"

**Message framework:**
> Hi [Name], I noticed you run [Company] — we've built a simple CRM tool specifically for cleaning businesses to manage client quotes, jobs and schedules without the spreadsheet chaos. I'd love to show you a 10-minute demo and get your honest thoughts. No obligation — we're in early access and looking for feedback from operators like you. Worth a quick call?

Keep it short. No screenshots, no feature lists, no pricing in the first message.

**Volume target:** 20 personalised messages per week. Track reply rate. Iterate the message every 2 weeks.

## 5. The Sales Conversation

At this stage, every "sales" conversation is actually a discovery + demo. The CCO (or founder) runs all of these personally.

### Structure (45 minutes)

**1. Discovery (15 mins)**
- How do you currently manage clients and jobs?
- Walk me through what happens when you send a quote to a new client
- What are the three biggest admin headaches in your business?
- Have you tried any tools? What happened?

**2. Demo (20 mins)**
- Show the workflow that maps to their exact pain (quote creation → send → client responds → job scheduled)
- Do NOT show every feature. Show the 3 things that solve their stated problems.
- Ask "does this look familiar?" and "would this actually help?" throughout

**3. Founding Member Ask (10 mins)**
- "We're looking for 5–8 businesses to be founding members. You'd get it free/discounted for 3 months, and in return we'd love 30 minutes of feedback from you each month."
- Handle objections honestly: "The product is still being refined — that's exactly why we want people like you involved"

### Demo Environment
- Use a populated demo account with realistic cleaning business data (not a blank screen)
- Have at least 3 sample clients, 5 jobs, and 2 quotes ready to show
- Test the PDF generation and share link before every call

## 6. Handling the "Polishing" Problem Honestly

The product is MVP. Some flows are rough. Billing is not live yet. The worst thing we can do is oversell and under-deliver to the first users who will set our reputation.

### What to Say

**On the product state:**
> "We're in early access — the core workflows are solid but we're actively improving the experience every week. Part of why we're talking to you is to make sure we build the right things."

**On billing:**
> "We're setting up our payment system now — for founding members, you'll be on a free or heavily reduced rate for the first 3 months while we finesse the billing experience."

**On missing features (e.g. Xero sync):**
> "That's on our roadmap — we're building it now. I'd rather be honest with you about what's live than promise something that isn't ready yet."

**On mobile:**
> "It works on mobile but we're refining it. If mobile-first is critical for your team, I want to make sure we're the right fit before you commit."

### Features That ARE Strong Enough to Sell On (Right Now)
- Quote creation and PDF generation
- Public quote sharing link (client-facing, professional)
- Activity timeline per client
- Follow-up task management
- Multi-property client management

### Features to Avoid Demonstrating Until Fixed
- Settings / team management (incomplete)
- Billing / plan enforcement (not live)
- Anything requiring mobile (until responsive work complete)

## 7. Onboarding First Users

Every founding member gets a structured onboarding. Do not leave them to self-serve.

### Week 1 Onboarding Flow

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| Day 0 | Send welcome email with direct Slack/WhatsApp invite. Include 2-min Loom video walkthrough of the product. |
| Day 1 | Kick-off call (30 mins). Walk through their first client setup together. |
| Day 3 | Check-in message: "How's it going? Any questions?" |
| Day 7 | First feedback call (20 mins, structured — see below). |
| Day 14 | Share what you've improved based on early feedback. Shows responsiveness. |
| Day 30 | First monthly review. Discuss sticking points, collect NPS informally. |

### First Feedback Call Template (Day 7)

1. What's working well?
2. What's confusing or frustrating?
3. Is there anything you've tried to do that you couldn't?
4. What would make you confident to recommend this to another cleaning business?
5. On a scale of 1–10, how likely are you to still be using this in 3 months?

Capture answers in a shared doc. Review weekly as a team.

## 8. Pricing Strategy for First Users

### Recommended Approach

| Stage | Price | Rationale |
|-------|-------|-----------|
| Founding Member (Cohort 1) | Free | Pre-billing infrastructure. Formalise with commitment letter. |
| Founding Member (Cohort 2) | £19/mo | Tests willingness to pay. Low enough to not feel risky. |
| Soft Launch | £39/mo Starter | Below final Starter price. Rewards early commitment. |
| Full Launch | £49/mo Starter | As per commercial pricing proposal. |

**Founding lifetime lock:** Offer cohort 1 and 2 members the right to stay on their founding rate as long as they remain active. This creates loyalty and reduces churn pressure early on.

### What NOT to Do
- Do not offer annual billing before the product is proven (people will want refunds)
- Do not charge before billing infrastructure is live and tested
- Do not discount so deeply that you attract price-sensitive users who won't give quality feedback

## 10. What Good Looks Like at 90 Days

By the end of 90 days from first outreach, we should be able to answer:

1. **Is there willingness to pay?** — At least 3 users expressing intent to pay, ideally already converted.
2. **What is the actual ICP?** — Do we now know *exactly* which type of cleaning business benefits most?
3. **What is the #1 blocker to conversion?** — Missing feature, price, trust, or something else?
4. **Is the product stable enough for paid launch?** — Zero P0 bugs, billing working, core flows polished.
5. **What does the referral rate look like?** — Have any founding members referred anyone? If yes, we have product-market fit signal.

## Appendix A: Objection Handling Quick Reference

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "We already use spreadsheets and it works fine" | "That's exactly who this is built for — the goal is to save you the 3–5 hours a week you spend maintaining them. Can I show you the quote flow in 5 minutes?" |
| "I've tried CRMs before and they're too complicated" | "Most CRMs are built for sales teams, not cleaning businesses. This is purpose-built for your specific workflow. It took one of our founding members 20 minutes to send their first quote." |
| "How much does it cost?" | "For founding members it's free/£19 for the first 3 months. We're still refining the product and I'd rather you experience it before committing to anything." |
| "What happens to my data?" | "Your data is yours. UK-hosted, encrypted, and you can export everything at any time. We're also working through our full GDPR compliance documentation." |
| "Does it integrate with Xero?" | "Not yet — that's on our near-term roadmap. If that's a hard requirement right now, I want to be upfront: it's not ready. But if you can manage with manual reconciliation for 3 months, we'd love your input on how the integration should work." |
| "Is it stable enough to rely on?" | "The core quote and client management workflows are solid — that's what our founding members are using daily. Some parts of the product are still being improved, which is why we're talking to people like you before a full launch." |

## Appendix B: UK Cleaning Industry Context

- Estimated 33,000+ cleaning businesses registered in the UK (Companies House)
- Market dominated by sole traders and micro-businesses (1–10 staff)
- Typical monthly revenue for small operator: £5,000–£30,000
- Key pain points: Quote-to-cash process, staff scheduling, client communication, cancellation management
- High staff turnover creates operational pressure — scheduling tools are valued
- Most operators are NOT digitally sophisticated — simplicity is a selling point, not just nice-to-have
- Price sensitivity is real — validate that £49/mo is viable by testing £19–£29 first
- Word-of-mouth is the dominant marketing channel in this sector — referrals will compound fast if product is good
