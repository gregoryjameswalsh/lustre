## 1. Brand Foundation

### 1.1 Who We Are

Lustre is the CRM and operations platform built specifically for UK cleaning and property maintenance businesses. We replace spreadsheets, WhatsApp threads, and generic software with a purpose-built system that makes professional operations accessible to every cleaning business — from a sole trader to a 200-operative enterprise.

### 1.2 What We Believe

We believe small and mid-sized cleaning businesses deserve software as good as the tools the big FM enterprises use — without the enterprise price tag, US-centric assumptions, or six-month implementation project.

We believe the cleaning industry is underestimated, and we're here to change that. The professionals who keep buildings clean and maintained deserve a professional tool.

### 1.3 Our Promise

> **"Run your cleaning business from one place."**

Every quote sent. Every job scheduled. Every client remembered. Every invoice tracked. One platform. Built for Britain. Built for the trade.

### 1.4 Positioning Statement

*For UK cleaning and property maintenance businesses that are ready to operate professionally, Lustre is the field-service CRM that replaces spreadsheets and generic software with a purpose-built, UK-native platform — unlike ServiceTitan or Jobber, which are built for the US or Australian market and carry complexity and cost that doesn't fit UK SMEs.*

### 1.5 Brand Personality

Lustre is:

| Trait | What it means in practice |
|---|---|
| **Precise** | We say what we mean. No vague promises, no marketing fluff. Our copy is specific. |
| **Confident** | We know this market. We don't hedge or apologise. We lead with conviction. |
| **Human** | We build for real people running real businesses, not abstract "users". |
| **British** | UK spelling, UK tax law, UK culture. We're not a US product pretending to be local. |
| **Trustworthy** | We ship what we promise. Enterprise-grade security isn't a future roadmap item — it's table stakes. |

Lustre is **not**:
- Startup casual (no slang, no hyperbole, no "10x your business")
- Corporate cold (no passive voice, no jargon walls, no "synergistic solutions")
- Generic SaaS (we are not "the platform for modern teams" — we are for cleaning businesses, specifically)

## 2. Logo System

### 2.1 The Mark

The Lustre mark is the **✦ diamond-star symbol** — a four-pointed star with precise geometry. It represents the lustre (shine, quality, finish) that cleaning professionals deliver, and the precision of our platform.

The mark is always rendered in **vector** — never rasterised at small sizes. Minimum digital size: **20px** (icon-only). Minimum print size: **6mm**.

### 2.2 Wordmark

The wordmark is set in **Instrument Serif**, lowercase, tracking at `0`. The combination of a geometric mark with a serif wordmark creates the contrast that makes the brand memorable — modern precision meets established authority.

```
✦  lustre
```

The mark and wordmark are separated by **1× the cap-height** of the wordmark at all sizes.

### 2.3 Logo Variants

| Variant | Usage |
|---|---|
| **Primary** — mark + wordmark, horizontal | Default. Navigation headers, email signatures, business cards |
| **Stacked** — mark above wordmark | Square formats: app icons, social profile images, document covers |
| **Mark only** — ✦ alone | Favicons, watermarks, embossed/debossed use, where wordmark is already established |
| **Wordmark only** | Long horizontal formats where mark has been used nearby |

### 2.4 Colour Variants

| Variant | Background | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| **Full colour** | White or light surface | Primary use |
| **Deep Forest** | White or light surface | Print, formal documents |
| **Reversed white** | Dark Forest (`#1A3329`) or Graphite | Dark headers, footers, print on dark stock |
| **Reversed Frost Mint** | Deep Forest | App dark mode |
| **Single colour black** | Any light background | Single-colour print, fax, legal documents |

### 2.5 Clear Space

Minimum clear space = **the height of the ✦ mark** on all four sides. Nothing — no other logo, text, or graphic element — may appear within this zone.

### 2.6 Misuse — Do Not

- Do not rotate the mark
- Do not stretch or distort proportions
- Do not apply drop shadows or glows
- Do not use on busy photographic backgrounds without a containing shape
- Do not recreate the wordmark in a different typeface
- Do not use the mark in a colour that isn't from the approved palette
- Do not place the logo on a background with insufficient contrast (minimum 4.5:1 ratio)

### 3.5 Accessibility

All text/background combinations must meet **WCAG 2.1 AA** minimum:
- Normal text (< 18pt / < 14pt bold): contrast ratio ≥ 4.5:1
- Large text (≥ 18pt / ≥ 14pt bold): contrast ratio ≥ 3:1
- UI components and graphical objects: contrast ratio ≥ 3:1

**Pre-verified combinations:**
- Graphite `#111827` on White `#FFFFFF` — **16.1:1** ✓
- Graphite `#111827` on Off-white `#F9FAFB` — **15.3:1** ✓
- White `#FFFFFF` on Deep Forest `#1A3329` — **12.8:1** ✓
- White `#FFFFFF` on Emerald `#3D7A5F` — **4.7:1** ✓ (large text / buttons only — verify at use)
- Graphite `#111827` on Frost Mint `#C8F5D7` — **9.2:1** ✓
- Harvest Gold text: use on White or Off-white only — `#C98A1A` on `#FFFFFF` = **4.6:1** ✓

Never use Sage `#7CB99A` for text on white — contrast is 2.9:1 (fail). Reserve for decorative use only.

## 4. Typography

### 4.1 Type Families

**Instrument Serif** — Headlines and display text
Source: Google Fonts (free, open licence)
Use: H1–H3, marketing headlines, pull quotes, hero text, document cover titles
Character: Elegant, modern serif with ink-trap details. Signals trust and establishment without feeling dated.

**Inter** — Body, UI, and functional text
Source: Google Fonts (free, open licence)
Use: H4–H6, body copy, UI labels, buttons, navigation, table data, form fields
Character: Clean, legible at small sizes, excellent for dense UI. Already in use — retain for continuity.

**JetBrains Mono** — Data and reference text
Source: JetBrains (free, open licence)
Use: Invoice and quote reference numbers, job IDs, API keys, code snippets, financial figures in PDFs
Character: Professional, legible tabular mono. Signals precision in data output.

### 4.2 Type Scale

All sizes defined in `rem` (base 16px). Line heights given as unitless multipliers.

| Token | Size | Line height | Weight | Family | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| `display` | 3.5rem / 56px | 1.1 | 700 | Instrument Serif | Hero headline (landing page only) |
| `h1` | 2.75rem / 44px | 1.15 | 700 | Instrument Serif | Page titles, section headlines |
| `h2` | 2rem / 32px | 1.2 | 700 | Instrument Serif | Section sub-headings, card titles |
| `h3` | 1.5rem / 24px | 1.25 | 600 | Instrument Serif | Feature titles, sidebar headings |
| `h4` | 1.25rem / 20px | 1.3 | 600 | Inter | UI section labels, modal titles |
| `h5` | 1.0625rem / 17px | 1.4 | 600 | Inter | Table headers, form group labels |
| `h6` | 0.875rem / 14px | 1.5 | 600 | Inter | Metadata labels, nav items |
| `body-lg` | 1.125rem / 18px | 1.7 | 400 | Inter | Landing page body copy |
| `body` | 1rem / 16px | 1.6 | 400 | Inter | App body copy, descriptions |
| `body-sm` | 0.9375rem / 15px | 1.6 | 400 | Inter | Secondary descriptions, captions |
| `label` | 0.875rem / 14px | 1.5 | 500 | Inter | Form labels, table cells |
| `caption` | 0.8125rem / 13px | 1.5 | 400 | Inter | Timestamps, helper text, footnotes |
| `overline` | 0.75rem / 12px | 1.5 | 600 | Inter | Section labels (uppercase, +0.08em tracking) |
| `mono` | 0.875rem / 14px | 1.5 | 400 | JetBrains Mono | IDs, codes, financial reference numbers |

### 4.3 Typography Rules

**Letter spacing:**
- Display and H1: `letter-spacing: -0.025em`
- H2: `letter-spacing: -0.02em`
- H3–H4: `letter-spacing: -0.015em`
- H5–H6: `letter-spacing: 0`
- Overline: `letter-spacing: +0.08em` (always uppercase)
- Body and below: `letter-spacing: 0` (Inter handles its own spacing well)

**Measure (line length):**
- Body copy maximum: 70 characters / 680px
- Sidebar and narrow column body: 45–55 characters
- UI labels and table cells: no max (content-determined)

**Vertical rhythm:**
- Paragraph spacing: `1em` (one body line)
- Section spacing multiples of `8px` (4px base unit × 2)

**Responsive scaling:**
- Display and H1: `clamp(2rem, 5vw, 3.5rem)` — scale between mobile and desktop
- H2: `clamp(1.5rem, 3.5vw, 2rem)`
- Body: never below `1rem` / 16px

## 8. Motion

### 8.1 Principles

Motion should be **functional, not decorative**. Every animation must help the user understand a change in state or guide their attention. We do not animate for the sake of it.

Lustre's motion language is calm and professional — like the product itself. No bouncing, no overshoot, no dramatic entrances. Elements should feel like they slide into place, not spring.

### 8.2 Duration Tokens

| Token | Duration | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| `duration-instant` | 75ms | Micro-interactions: checkbox tick, toggle flip |
| `duration-fast` | 150ms | Button hover/focus states, input borders |
| `duration-normal` | 200ms | Dropdown open, tooltip appear, badge transition |
| `duration-slow` | 300ms | Modal enter, slide-out panels, page transitions |
| `duration-deliberate` | 400ms | Onboarding step transitions, feature reveals |

### 8.3 Easing

| Token | Value | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| `ease-default` | `cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1)` | Default for most UI transitions (ease-out-expo feel) |
| `ease-in` | `cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 1, 1)` | Elements leaving the screen |
| `ease-out` | `cubic-bezier(0, 0, 0.2, 1)` | Elements entering the screen |
| `ease-linear` | `linear` | Progress bars, loading states |

### 8.4 Standard Transitions

```css
/* Button hover */
transition: background-color 150ms ease-default, box-shadow 150ms ease-default;

/* Dropdown / popover */
transition: opacity 200ms ease-out, transform 200ms ease-out;
/* Enter: opacity 0→1, translateY 4px→0 */

/* Modal */
transition: opacity 300ms ease-out;
/* Backdrop: opacity 0→0.5 */

/* Sidebar collapse */
transition: width 300ms ease-default;

/* Input focus border */
transition: border-color 150ms ease-default, box-shadow 150ms ease-default;
```

### 8.5 Reduced Motion

Always respect `prefers-reduced-motion`:

```css
@media (prefers-reduced-motion: reduce) {
  *, *::before, *::after {
    animation-duration: 0.01ms !important;
    transition-duration: 0.01ms !important;
  }
}
```

## 9. Photography & Illustration

### 9.1 Photography Style

Lustre uses photography of **real working environments** — not stock-photo clichés. We show cleaning professionals doing skilled work, not posed actors in pristine environments.

**Directorial principles:**
- Show the work being done well — confidence and competence, not subservience
- Natural light or site-appropriate lighting — not artificially bright studio settings
- Diverse team compositions reflecting real UK cleaning businesses
- Equipment and locations that feel genuine (professional cleaning machinery, real office/commercial spaces, residential properties)
- Colour grade: slightly cool, clean, with lifted shadows — precise but warm

**What to avoid:**
- Overly staged "team meeting" shots with everyone smiling at the camera
- US-looking environments (wrong plug sockets, signage, vehicles)
- Images where the cleaning is the punchline rather than the profession
- Excessive lens flare, bokeh, or moody darkness

### 9.2 Screenshots & Product Imagery

Product screenshots are a core part of our marketing. Rules:

- Always use real data (or realistic anonymised data) — never placeholder "Lorem ipsum" content
- Crop to show the most compelling part of the interface — do not show the full browser chrome unless demonstrating responsive design
- Add a `border-radius: 12px` container with `shadow-xl` when floating on a light background
- On dark section backgrounds, use the screenshot directly without extra container styling
- Keep screenshots current — outdated UI in marketing material damages trust

### 9.3 Illustration

If illustration is used (empty states, onboarding, error pages):

- Style: **Line illustration** — clean, geometric, minimal colour
- Palette: Pulls from brand palette only (Deep Forest, Emerald, Sage, Frost Mint) — no unrelated colours
- Character style: Simple, professional, non-cartoonish
- Never use generic off-the-shelf illustration sets without brand colour adaptation

## 10. Voice & Tone Guide

### 10.1 Principles

**Be specific.** Never say "powerful features" — say "send a quote in under two minutes." Specificity builds credibility; vagueness destroys it.

**Be direct.** Lead with the point. Background and context come second, if at all. Our users are busy business owners, not content browsers.

**Be British.** UK spelling throughout. Acknowledge UK-specific realities (VAT, GDPR, bank holidays). Avoid Americanisms.

**Be human.** We are building for real people. Use "you" and "your". Acknowledge that running a business is genuinely hard.

**Be confident without being arrogant.** We know this market better than any US or Australian competitor. We don't need to shout — we just need to be precise and true.

### 10.2 UK Spelling Reference

Always use UK spellings:

| US (wrong) | UK (correct) |
|---|---|
| organize | organise |
| customize | customise |
| optimize | optimise |
| color | colour |
| center | centre |
| license (noun) | licence |
| program (software) | programme *(but "software program" is acceptable)* |
| traveling | travelling |
| focused | focussed *(acceptable either way — be consistent)* |
| labor | labour |
| analyze | analyse |

Always use: £ (not $), VAT (not sales tax), postcode (not zip code), mobile (not cell phone), holiday (not vacation).

### 10.3 Tone Modulation by Context

The core brand voice is consistent, but the tone shifts by context:

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Landing page hero | Confident, inspiring | "Run your cleaning business from one place." |
| Landing page feature copy | Specific, benefit-led | "Send a branded quote PDF in two minutes. Clients click to accept — no paper, no back-and-forth." |
| Onboarding | Warm, guiding | "Let's get your first client added. It takes about 30 seconds." |
| Error messages | Clear, not apologetic | "We couldn't save this quote. Check your connection and try again." |
| Empty states | Encouraging, action-oriented | "No clients yet. Add your first one and you'll be ready to send quotes." |
| Enterprise sales materials | Authoritative, precise | "Lustre is ISO-ready, GDPR-compliant by design, and supports SAML 2.0 SSO via WorkOS." |
| Email notifications | Efficient, friendly | "A new quote has been accepted. Sarah Chen has accepted Quote #QT-0042 for £1,840 + VAT." |
| In-app success messages | Brief, positive | "Quote sent." / "Job scheduled." / "Client added." |

### 10.4 Words to Avoid

| Avoid | Use instead |
|---|---|
| "Powerful" | Specific capability ("handles 500+ clients") |
| "Seamless" | Describe the actual experience |
| "Leverage" | Use |
| "Synergy" | (Never) |
| "Game-changer" | (Never) |
| "Revolutionise" | (Reserved for major announcements only, use sparingly) |
| "Easy" | Describe why it's easy — "three clicks to send a quote" |
| "Simple" | Same — specificity over adjectives |
| "World-class" | Let the product demonstrate it |
| "Users" (for customers) | "Your team", "cleaning businesses", "you" |
| "Utilize" | Use |
| "Functionality" | Features, tools, capabilities |

**Hero headline options (Instrument Serif, H1):**
